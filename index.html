WHO I AM

Over the course of a decade I have negotiated national television buys at Optimedia (now Blue 449), ran the Media Strategy, East team at the demand-side platform TubeMogul (acquired by Adobe) and now drive product development and marketing for Alliant’s suite of data solutions. I believe in bringing together technical expertise and creativity to produce innovative product and campaign strategies. With thousands of buyers, across hundreds of brands and agencies, I strive to bring them products and services that meet their evolving needs.

A graduate of the University at Albany, SUNY School of Business, I now reside in Westchester county, NY. Outside of work you can find me spending time with my wife and daughters, as well as our Australian shepherd Bruiser (despite his name he is quite a softie). On the other days you will likely find me out on the golf course, taking some turns on a ski hill, or on a hiking trail somewhere. While a lot of my hobbies focus on the outdoors, I also enjoy photography, trying out new restaurants and reading about a wide variety of topics. During commutes I'm typically listening to podcasts that educate, ignite curiosity, challenge traditional thinking and encourage people to push themselves.

The more product & marketing managers can understand and empathize with the various stakeholders they partner with, the more successful they will be. Throughout my 10+ year career I have held roles on both the sales and buy side of the advertising market. Starting out as a buyer, executing media buys for major national advertisers allowed me to gain deep understanding of a client's mindset. Once on the sales side I was able to focus on strategy, packaging and positioning to best address client needs. It was during this time that I discovered a natural tendency to see gaps and partner across teams to develop solutions. It was this realization that ultimately led me to product-focused positions.

No matter the role, my goal is to always remain curious, face challenges head on and try to be better each day.

PROJECTS

Mastercard's CMO, Raja Rajamannar, was recently quoted saying “There is such a significant component of technology within marketing that without understanding how to leverage it, you will not be able to do your job.” For this reason I am constantly observing how evolving technologies, such as cloud platforms and CDPs, are impacting how businesses operate. To take things a step further, I also wanted to expand my technical skill set by learning how to code. Beginning with basic HTML and CSS, then expanding to more advanced areas such as JavaScript and Python. While my role does not require me to code on a regular basis, understanding areas such as front and back-end development, frameworks, and databases has had a tremendous impact on the dialogue I have with technical teams and my ability to communicate product developments more effectively.

One of the best ways to solidify knowledge is to get hands on. It's why I built this site from scratch and why I created sample projects to apply new-found skills. Click on an image to view more information about each project.
